Professional Engineers as Expert Witnesses in Patent Infringement Cases in Missouri

April 7, 2026

Patent infringement cases often involve complex technical issues that are not easily understood without specialized knowledge. In these situations, a Professional Engineer (PE) may be engaged as an expert witness to help explain how a product, system, or process works and how it compares to a patented invention.

In Missouri, engineering expert witnesses can play an important role in clarifying technical facts for attorneys, judges, and juries.

This article provides general information about the role of a Professional Engineer as an expert witness in patent infringement matters.

What Is an Engineering Expert Witness?

An engineering expert witness is a professional who provides independent technical opinions in legal proceedings. Their role is to:

  • Analyze technical evidence
  • Apply engineering principles to real-world systems
  • Explain complex concepts in understandable terms
  • Provide written reports and, if needed, testimony

In patent cases, this often involves comparing a patented design to an accused product or process.

Why Engineers Are Important in Patent Cases

Patent disputes typically involve questions such as:

  • Does the accused product contain the elements described in the patent claims?
  • How does the system function in practice?
  • Are differences between designs meaningful or superficial?
  • What would a person skilled in the field understand from the patent?

These are technical questions that benefit from engineering analysis.

Role of a Professional Engineer in Infringement Analysis

A PE serving as an expert witness may perform tasks such as:

1️⃣ Reviewing Patent Claims and Technical Documents

Engineers may analyze:

  • Patent claims and specifications
  • Technical drawings and schematics
  • Product documentation
  • Prior art references

The goal is to understand how the patented system is defined and implemented.

2️⃣ Comparing Systems or Products

A key part of infringement analysis is comparing:

  • The patented invention
  • The accused product or process

This comparison may involve identifying:

  • Similar components or features
  • Functional similarities
  • Differences in structure or operation

3️⃣ Evaluating Technical Equivalence

In some cases, the question is not whether systems are identical, but whether they are functionally equivalent.

Engineering analysis may consider:

  • Whether components perform substantially the same function
  • Whether they operate in a similar way
  • Whether differences materially affect performance

4️⃣ Reverse Engineering and Testing

Where appropriate, engineers may:

  • Inspect physical products
  • Conduct measurements or testing
  • Analyze system behavior

This helps ensure conclusions are based on actual performance rather than assumptions.

5️⃣ Preparing Expert Reports

Engineering expert witnesses often prepare written reports that:

  • Define the scope of analysis
  • Summarize materials reviewed
  • Explain methodologies used
  • Present technical opinions
  • Identify assumptions and limitations

Clear documentation is important for both legal review and court proceedings.

6️⃣ Providing Testimony

If a case proceeds to deposition or trial, a PE may:

  • Explain technical findings under oath
  • Respond to questions from attorneys
  • Clarify complex concepts for non-technical audiences

Importance of Objectivity

An engineering expert witness is expected to provide independent and objective analysis. Their role is not to advocate, but to:

  • Evaluate evidence using engineering principles
  • Base conclusions on data and analysis
  • Clearly communicate findings

Objectivity is critical to credibility in legal settings.

Distinction Between Engineering and Legal Conclusions

It is important to distinguish roles:

  • Engineers evaluate technical function and comparison
  • Attorneys and courts determine legal infringement and liability

Engineering analysis supports legal arguments but does not replace legal judgment.

When to Engage an Engineering Expert

In Missouri, a Professional Engineer may be helpful in patent cases when:

  • The subject matter involves complex technical systems
  • There is disagreement about how a product functions
  • Technical comparisons are central to the case
  • An expert opinion is required for litigation
  • Independent analysis is needed early in the case

Early engagement can help identify strengths and weaknesses in technical arguments.

Missouri Context

Professional Engineers in Missouri are licensed to provide engineering services within their area of competence. When acting as expert witnesses, they may provide opinions based on their training, experience, and analysis of available evidence.

Their work is often used in conjunction with legal counsel and may be subject to applicable rules governing expert testimony.

Closing

Professional Engineers can play a valuable role in patent infringement cases by providing clear, technically grounded analysis of how systems function and how designs compare. In Missouri, engineering expert witnesses help bridge the gap between complex technical concepts and legal decision-making.
