Forensic Engineering Reports for Drainage Culverts in Missouri: Erosion, Structural Damage, and When a Professional Engineer Can Help

May 3, 2026

Drainage issues can create significant property and infrastructure problems over time. In Missouri, improperly designed, damaged, obstructed, or undersized culverts may contribute to erosion, settlement, flooding, or structural distress affecting roads, driveways, retaining features, foundations, and adjacent land.

When questions arise about whether a culvert or drainage system is contributing to damage, a Professional Engineer (PE) may be engaged to perform a forensic engineering evaluation.

This article provides general information about drainage-related forensic engineering and when engineering analysis may be useful.

What Is a Drainage Culvert?

A culvert is a structure that conveys water beneath:

  • Roads
  • Driveways
  • Rail crossings
  • Embankments
  • Access paths

Common culvert types include:

  • Corrugated metal pipe
  • Reinforced concrete pipe
  • Box culverts
  • HDPE or plastic pipe systems

Their purpose is to manage drainage flow while supporting overlying loads.

Common Problems Associated with Culverts

Culvert issues can lead to property damage or infrastructure concerns such as:

Erosion and Scour

Water exiting or bypassing a culvert may cause:

  • Soil loss around inlets or outlets
  • Channel erosion
  • Undermining of slopes or embankments

Over time, this can destabilize adjacent structures or land.

Settlement or Sinkholes

Improper drainage or washout may contribute to:

  • Settlement of roadways or driveways
  • Voids beneath slabs or pavements
  • Localized sinkholes or depressions

These conditions may indicate subsurface soil movement.

Structural Damage

Drainage-related movement may affect nearby structures, including:

  • Foundations
  • Retaining walls
  • Pavements
  • Driveways
  • Utility crossings

In some cases, damage may be gradual and not immediately obvious.

Flooding or Overtopping

Undersized or obstructed culverts may fail to convey design flow, leading to:

  • Localized flooding
  • Overtopping of roads or access paths
  • Water intrusion onto adjacent properties

What a Forensic Engineering Report May Evaluate

A Professional Engineer performing a drainage or culvert forensic review may assess:

  • Existing culvert configuration and condition
  • Site grading and drainage paths
  • Evidence of erosion, scour, or soil migration
  • Structural distress or settlement patterns
  • Water flow behavior and discharge conditions
  • Apparent relationship between drainage behavior and observed damage

The scope depends on the assignment and available site information.

Common Reasons a Forensic Report Is Requested

In Missouri, drainage-related forensic reports may be useful when:

  • Property damage is believed to be caused by drainage changes
  • A culvert failure affects access or stability
  • Erosion is threatening structures or improvements
  • There is disagreement regarding cause of damage
  • Repair scope or responsibility is disputed
  • Legal, insurance, or permitting documentation is needed

Engineering documentation can help clarify technical conditions.

How a Professional Engineer Can Help

A PE may assist by:

1️⃣ Evaluating Cause and Contributing Factors

Engineering review may help determine whether drainage conditions plausibly contributed to observed issues.

2️⃣ Documenting Site Conditions

A report may include observations, measurements, photographs, and technical findings.

3️⃣ Identifying Potential Failure Mechanisms

Examples include:

  • Undermining from scour
  • Hydraulic overtopping
  • Inlet or outlet blockage
  • Progressive erosion or washout

4️⃣ Supporting Repair Planning

Engineering analysis may help define:

  • Drainage improvements
  • Erosion control strategies
  • Culvert replacement or resizing considerations
  • Stabilization approaches

5️⃣ Providing Technical Documentation

Reports may support:

  • Insurance review
  • Legal disputes
  • Property negotiations
  • Permit or municipal discussions

Missouri Drainage Considerations

Missouri properties commonly experience drainage challenges due to:

  • Heavy rainfall events
  • Variable soil conditions
  • Steep grades or embankments
  • Rural and semi-rural access crossings
  • Aging infrastructure

These conditions can increase the importance of proper drainage management.

Practical Steps If You Suspect Culvert-Related Damage

If you suspect a drainage or culvert issue:

  • Document visible conditions with photos
  • Note changes after rainfall events
  • Avoid altering the site before evaluation when possible
  • Track erosion progression or settlement areas
  • Consider whether engineering review is warranted

Preserving site conditions can be helpful for technical analysis.

Closing

Drainage culverts play an important role in managing stormwater and protecting infrastructure. In Missouri, problems involving erosion, settlement, flooding, or structural damage may warrant forensic engineering review to better understand what is occurring and what actions may be appropriate.

A Professional Engineer can help evaluate conditions, document findings, and provide technical analysis relevant to drainage-related concerns.
