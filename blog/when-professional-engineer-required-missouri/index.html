When Is a Professional Engineer Required in Missouri?

Understanding When Engineering Licensure Is Necessary

In Missouri, the practice of professional engineering is regulated to protect public health, safety, and welfare. Not every technical task requires a licensed Professional Engineer (PE), but certain activities legally do.

Understanding when a PE is required can help project owners, developers, contractors, and attorneys avoid delays, compliance issues, or rejected submissions.

What Constitutes the Practice of Engineering in Missouri

Missouri law defines the practice of engineering broadly. In general, it includes the application of engineering principles and judgment to services such as:

  • Engineering analysis and evaluation
  • Preparation of engineering drawings and specifications
  • Design or review of systems affecting safety or performance
  • Engineering reports, opinions, or certifications

If a task involves professional judgment that could impact public safety, it may fall under regulated engineering practice.

Common Situations Where a PE Is Required

While requirements vary by jurisdiction and project type, a Missouri-licensed PE is commonly required for:

  • Engineering drawings submitted to public agencies
  • Structural, mechanical, electrical, or system designs affecting safety
  • Sealed engineering calculations or reports
  • Expert engineering opinions in legal or insurance matters
  • Engineering evaluations relied upon by third parties

Local authorities may impose additional requirements depending on the scope and nature of the work.

Activities That May Not Require a PE

Not all technical work requires licensure. Examples of activities that may not require a PE include:

  • Drafting or modeling performed under supervision
  • Work based strictly on prescriptive codes without judgment
  • Internal technical analysis not released externally
  • Manufacturing or installation following stamped plans
  • Software development that does not constitute regulated engineering practice

The distinction often depends on whether professional judgment is exercised and whether the work is offered to the public.

Engineering Review vs. Engineering Design

Engineering review and engineering design are related but distinct activities.

  • Design involves creating engineering solutions and selecting parameters
  • Review involves evaluating work prepared by others for compliance, reasonableness, and suitability

Both activities may require licensure when professional judgment is applied and the work is relied upon by others.

Sealing and Responsibility

When a Missouri PE seals a document, they are affirming that:

  • The work was prepared by them or under their responsible charge
  • The work meets applicable standards and requirements
  • They accept professional responsibility for the content

Sealing is not a formality. It carries legal and ethical obligations and should only be done when appropriate.

Consequences of Improper Practice

Performing regulated engineering services without proper licensure can result in:

  • Rejected submissions or project delays
  • Regulatory action or penalties
  • Increased liability exposure
  • Challenges to the validity of work products

Clarifying licensure requirements early can prevent issues later.

When to Seek Professional Guidance

If there is uncertainty about whether a PE is required, consulting with a licensed Professional Engineer early in the process can help clarify obligations and define appropriate scope.
