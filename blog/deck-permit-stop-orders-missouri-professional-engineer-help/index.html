Deck Permit Stop Orders in Missouri: How a Professional Engineer Can Help

April 15, 2026

Deck projects are common in Missouri, but they can run into issues when construction does not align with approved plans or permit requirements. In some cases, a building department may issue a stop work order, halting construction until concerns are resolved.

Whether you are dealing with a stop order or want to avoid one, a licensed Professional Engineer (PE) can help evaluate the situation and provide technical guidance.

This article provides general information about deck-related stop orders and how engineering review may assist before or after one is issued.

What Is a Stop Work Order?

A stop work order is issued by a local building department to pause construction when:

  • Work is being done without a required permit
  • Construction does not match approved plans
  • Code compliance issues are identified
  • Inspections were not completed at required stages

Once issued, work typically cannot continue until the identified issues are addressed and approval is granted.

Common Reasons Deck Projects Receive Stop Orders

Decks may be flagged for issues such as:

  • Improper attachment to the house (e.g., ledger board concerns)
  • Insufficient or improperly sized footings
  • Over-spanned beams or joists
  • Missing or incorrect connectors and fasteners
  • Inadequate guardrails or stair construction
  • Deviations from approved plans

Even small deviations can trigger a stop order if they affect safety or code compliance.

How a Professional Engineer Can Help Before a Stop Order

Engaging a Professional Engineer early in the project can help reduce the likelihood of issues by:

  • Reviewing design drawings before submission
  • Evaluating structural components such as beams, joists, and connections
  • Identifying potential code compliance concerns
  • Providing engineered details when prescriptive methods are not sufficient

Early review can help ensure that plans are aligned with local requirements before construction begins.

How a Professional Engineer Can Help After a Stop Order

If a stop work order has already been issued, a PE may assist with:

1️⃣ Evaluating Existing Construction

The engineer can assess what has already been built and identify areas of concern.

2️⃣ Determining Structural Adequacy

An evaluation may determine whether elements can remain as built or require modification.

3️⃣ Developing Repair or Modification Plans

Engineering input may help define how to correct deficiencies, such as:

  • Reinforcing framing
  • Adjusting connections
  • Modifying footings or supports

4️⃣ Preparing Documentation for the Building Department

Some jurisdictions may accept engineered drawings or calculations to resolve non-prescriptive conditions.

5️⃣ Supporting Re-Inspection

Clear documentation can help facilitate re-inspection and approval to resume work.

When Engineering May Be Required

In Missouri, a Professional Engineer may be required when:

  • The deck design falls outside prescriptive code provisions
  • Structural elements are non-standard or modified
  • The building department requests engineered documentation
  • There is uncertainty about load capacity or safety
  • Repairs involve structural changes

Permit and Code Considerations

Deck construction in Missouri is typically governed by locally adopted versions of residential building codes. These codes often include prescriptive requirements for:

  • Joist spans and spacing
  • Beam sizing
  • Ledger attachment
  • Footing dimensions
  • Guardrail and stair construction

When a project does not meet prescriptive requirements, engineering analysis may be needed to demonstrate compliance.

Practical Steps If You Receive a Stop Order

If your deck project has been stopped:

  • Review the notice to understand the cited issues
  • Avoid continuing work until approval is granted
  • Document current conditions
  • Coordinate with your contractor and local building department
  • Consider whether engineering evaluation may help resolve the issue

Addressing concerns systematically can help move the project forward.

Closing

Stop work orders on deck projects can be disruptive, but they are intended to address safety and compliance concerns. In Missouri, a Professional Engineer can help evaluate construction, clarify technical issues, and provide documentation that supports resolution.

Whether engaged before or after a stop order, engineering input can help align a project with applicable requirements and support a path toward completion.
