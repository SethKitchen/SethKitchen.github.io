Understanding Permit Requirements in Wentzville, Missouri

May 27, 2026

Permits are a key part of property improvement, construction, and renovation projects in Wentzville. Whether you're building a new home, adding a deck, replacing major building systems, or making structural changes, knowing when a permit is required — and how the city's process works — can save time, prevent delays, and help ensure compliance with local codes.

This article provides general information about permit requirements in Wentzville, Missouri. It does not constitute legal or engineering advice or establish a professional engagement.

What Permits Are and Why They Matter

Permits are official approvals issued by the City of Wentzville's Community Development Department / Building Division that confirm a project complies with applicable building codes and local ordinances. Permits help ensure construction is safe, structurally sound, and consistent with public health and welfare standards.

Building permits are required to construct, modify, or in many cases repair a structure. Work of a minor nature — such as replacing a faucet — typically does not require a permit, but requirements vary by project type and scope.

SmartGov and the Digital Permit Process

Wentzville has moved to an all-digital application and plan review platform through the SmartGov Customer Portal. The city no longer accepts paper plan submittals; building, planning, and zoning applications must be submitted electronically.

As of early 2026, new building permit applications are processed through SmartGov. Through the portal, applicants can:

  • Apply for building and miscellaneous permits
  • Submit construction plans and supporting documents
  • Pay permit fees online
  • Request and track inspections
  • Look up permit status and inspection results
  • Download approved permits and related documents

New users must create a portal account before submitting an application. For questions about account setup or older permits, contact the Building Division at (636) 639-2121 or ContactUs@wentzvillemo.gov.

How the Permit Process Works

In Wentzville, the process generally includes:

  1. Application Submission: Permits are applied for through the SmartGov portal with required plans and documentation uploaded digitally.
  2. Plan Review: Plans examiners review submissions for compliance with codes and local standards. Review times vary by permit type — miscellaneous permits often take about five to seven business days, while new construction permits may take longer.
  3. Comments & Corrections: Staff may contact applicants if additional information or revisions are needed.
  4. Permit Issuance: Once approved, fees can be paid and the permit printed from the portal.
  5. Inspections: Construction must be inspected at required stages. Inspections can be scheduled through the portal.
  6. Final Approval: After final inspection and closeout, the project is complete in the system.

Permits can be turned around quickly when paperwork is accurate and complete. Incomplete or unclear submissions are a common source of delay.

Contractor Licensing

All contractors — general and subcontractors — are required to hold a City of Wentzville contractor license before performing work in the city. Licenses must be renewed annually by February 28. If a contractor's license is not valid at the time of permit review, permit processing may be delayed until licensing is resolved. Homeowners may perform minor work themselves, but excessive code violations noted during inspection may require a licensed contractor to complete corrections.

Common Projects That Require Permits

Many typical projects in Wentzville require permits. The city's published lists are not all-encompassing, but commonly permitted work includes:

  • New residential or commercial construction
  • Building additions and remodels
  • Accessory buildings and sheds
  • Decks and patio covers
  • Driveway installation and related curb cuts
  • Fences
  • Finished basements
  • Swimming pools and hot tubs
  • New or replaced electrical, plumbing, or HVAC systems
  • Retaining walls over 4 feet tall
  • Sewer and water line installation or repairs
  • Solar panel installations

Miscellaneous building permits cover many residential alteration projects. New home construction may require additional submittals such as soils reports, surveys, or specialty inspections depending on the project.

Examples of Projects That Often Do Not Require Permits

Not all work requires a formal permit. Projects that typically do not require one in Wentzville include:

  • Minor repairs that do not affect building systems or structure
  • Replacing a faucet or similar fixture changes
  • Patios or non-driveway concrete slabs (driveway slabs and curb cuts at intersections do require permits)

When in doubt, contact the Building Division before starting work. Even apparently minor projects can trigger permit requirements depending on scope, location, and applicable codes.

Permit Validity, Extensions, and After-the-Fact Permits

Building permits are generally valid for six months from the date of the last approved inspection. If work is suspended or abandoned for six months, a permit may become invalid. Occupancy permits are valid for 90 days and may be extended by contacting the Building Official.

Permits are also required for work completed without prior approval. The city can work with property owners on after-the-fact permits, though inspectors may note that underlying concealed work was not inspected during construction. Some corrections may be required to meet current code.

Inspections and Fees

Inspections are scheduled through the SmartGov portal at required stages of construction. Permit fees are based on estimated construction cost and permit type, with separate plan review fees for many miscellaneous permits. Utility connection fees, deposits, and engineering-related charges may apply in addition to building permit fees for new construction.

Extra inspection fees may apply when multiple re-inspections are required. Fee schedules are published by the Building Division and should be confirmed at the time of application.

When Engineered Plans May Be Required

Wentzville plan reviewers may request plans prepared or sealed by a licensed Professional Engineer when:

  • Structural elements are added, removed, or altered
  • Design falls outside prescriptive code provisions
  • Commercial or multi-family buildings are involved
  • Retaining walls, foundations, or load-bearing changes are proposed
  • Mechanical, electrical, or plumbing scope exceeds typical residential limits

Complete, code-compliant documentation at submittal can reduce review cycles and help avoid stop-work situations later.

Why Permits Matter to Property Owners

Proper permitting helps ensure:

  • Construction meets safety and code requirements
  • Inspections occur at appropriate stages
  • Future buyers can verify compliant improvements
  • Insurance claims are supported by documented approvals

Work done without required permits may result in enforcement action, penalties, or difficulties selling or refinancing a property.

Practical Tips for Permit Applicants

To help your permit process go smoothly in Wentzville:

  • Confirm permit requirements with the Building Division before starting work.
  • Register in SmartGov and gather required documents from the portal's document center before applying.
  • Verify contractor licenses are current before submittal.
  • Provide clear, complete digital plans and descriptions.
  • Respond promptly to review comments.
  • Schedule inspections at the required stages.
  • Keep permit and inspection records for future use.
