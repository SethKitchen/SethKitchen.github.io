Installing Floor Joists: Signs of Poor Workmanship and When to Involve a Professional Engineer in Kansas City

April 6, 2026

Floor joists are a primary structural component in residential and light commercial construction. They support floors, transfer loads to beams and walls, and help maintain overall building stability. When joists are installed correctly, they typically perform without issue. When they are not, problems may appear immediately or develop over time.

In Kansas City, Missouri, concerns about joist installation may arise during construction, after a remodel, or when unusual floor behavior is observed.

This article provides general information about joist installation, signs of potential issues, and when a Professional Engineer (PE) may be involved.

What Floor Joists Do

Floor joists span between supports, such as beams or load-bearing walls, and carry loads from:

  • Occupants and furniture
  • Flooring materials
  • Interior walls
  • Mechanical systems

Proper installation is important for stiffness, strength, and long-term performance.

Basic Joist Installation Considerations

Correct joist installation generally involves:

  • Proper sizing and spacing
  • Adequate bearing at supports
  • Correct use of joist hangers or ledger connections
  • Limiting notches and holes within allowable ranges
  • Alignment and leveling across the floor system

When these elements are not addressed correctly, performance issues may occur.

Signs That Joist Work May Not Have Been Done Well

Uneven or Bouncy Floors

Floors that feel soft, excessively flexible, or uneven may indicate:

  • Joists that are undersized or over-spanned
  • Improper spacing
  • Lack of adequate support

Visible Sagging or Deflection

Noticeable sagging in the middle of a span can suggest:

  • Overloading
  • Insufficient joist size
  • Long-term creep or movement

Improper Notching or Drilling

Joists may be compromised if:

  • Notches are too deep or located near mid-span
  • Holes are oversized or placed incorrectly
  • Multiple modifications weaken the member

Poor Connections at Supports

Connections are critical. Problems may include:

  • Missing or incorrect joist hangers
  • Improper fasteners
  • Inadequate bearing length
  • Loose or misaligned connections

Cracking, Splitting, or Damage

Joists may show:

  • Longitudinal splitting
  • Cracks near supports or notches
  • Damage from construction or moisture exposure

Modifications After Original Construction

Issues sometimes arise when joists are altered during:

  • Plumbing or HVAC installation
  • Electrical routing
  • Remodeling projects

Uncoordinated modifications can reduce capacity.

Kansas City Considerations

In Kansas City, joist performance may be influenced by:

  • Seasonal moisture changes affecting wood
  • Long spans in newer open-concept homes
  • Remodels involving removal of interior walls
  • Code requirements adopted by the local jurisdiction

Local permitting and inspection processes may require documentation for structural modifications.

When a Professional Engineer May Be Needed

Structural Concerns Are Observed

  • Sagging floors
  • Excessive deflection
  • Visible damage to joists

Load-Bearing Elements Are Modified

  • Removal of walls
  • Addition of new loads such as tile, equipment, or partitions

Permit or Inspection Requirements Apply

  • Building departments may request engineered details
  • Non-standard conditions may require justification

Repairs or Reinforcement Are Needed

Engineering input may help determine:

  • Whether joists can remain in service
  • Appropriate reinforcement methods such as sistering or adding beams
  • Load redistribution strategies

Disputes or Quality Concerns Arise

If there is disagreement about whether work was performed correctly, an independent engineering evaluation can provide technical clarity.

What an Engineering Evaluation May Include

A joist-related evaluation may involve:

  • Visual inspection of framing
  • Measurement of spans, spacing, and deflection
  • Review of construction or remodel history
  • Identification of potential failure modes
  • Recommendations for repair or further investigation

The scope depends on the specific situation.

Practical Guidance for Property Owners

If you suspect issues with joist installation:

  • Document visible conditions with photos
  • Avoid making additional structural changes
  • Confirm whether a permit was issued for recent work
  • Consider obtaining an independent evaluation before proceeding with repairs

Early assessment can help prevent further damage or unnecessary work.

Closing

Proper joist installation is essential for safe and functional floor systems. In Kansas City, Missouri, signs such as uneven floors, sagging, or improper modifications may indicate underlying issues that warrant further evaluation.

A Professional Engineer can help assess conditions, determine whether the structure is performing as expected, and provide guidance on appropriate next steps.
