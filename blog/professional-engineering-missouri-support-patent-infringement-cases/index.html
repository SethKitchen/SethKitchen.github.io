How Professional Engineering in Missouri Can Support Patent Infringement Cases

April 1, 2026

Patent disputes often hinge on technical details, such as how a device works, how a system is constructed, or whether one design meaningfully differs from another. In these situations, engineering analysis can play an important role in clarifying complex technical issues for attorneys, courts, and decision-makers.

In Missouri, licensed Professional Engineers (PEs) may be engaged to provide independent technical evaluation in matters involving alleged patent infringement.

This article provides general information about how engineering expertise can support patent-related cases.

Why Engineering Matters in Patent Disputes

Patent infringement cases frequently involve questions such as:

  • How does the accused product or system function?
  • Does it operate in a way that is consistent with the patent claims?
  • Are differences between designs technically significant?
  • What would a person skilled in the art understand from the patent?

Answering these questions often requires analysis grounded in engineering principles rather than general descriptions.

Understanding Patent Claims and Technical Scope

Patent claims define the scope of protection. Engineering analysis may assist in:

  • Interpreting how technical elements are implemented
  • Comparing claim language to real-world systems
  • Identifying whether specific components or functions are present
  • Evaluating whether differences are structural, functional, or both

While legal interpretation of claims is ultimately determined by the court, engineering input can help explain how those claims relate to physical systems.

Comparative Technical Analysis

A common task in infringement cases is comparing two designs:

  • The patented system
  • The accused product or process

Engineering analysis may involve:

  • Reviewing drawings, schematics, and specifications
  • Evaluating how each system operates
  • Identifying similarities and differences
  • Assessing whether differences affect function or performance

This type of comparison helps clarify whether two systems are technically equivalent or meaningfully distinct.

Reverse Engineering and System Evaluation

In some cases, a product may need to be examined directly. Engineering work may include:

  • Physical inspection of components
  • Measurement and documentation of system features
  • Evaluation of materials and configurations
  • Analysis of how the system performs under typical conditions

The goal is to understand the actual behavior of the system rather than relying solely on descriptions.

Role in Expert Witness Testimony

Professional Engineers may serve as expert witnesses in patent disputes. In that role, they may:

  • Prepare technical reports
  • Provide deposition or trial testimony
  • Explain engineering concepts in clear, accessible terms
  • Respond to opposing expert opinions

Courts often rely on expert testimony to interpret complex technical issues.

Distinguishing Engineering and Legal Roles

It is important to distinguish between engineering analysis and legal conclusions:

  • Engineers evaluate technical function, structure, and performance
  • Attorneys and courts determine legal infringement, claim scope, and remedies

Engineering opinions support the legal process but do not replace it.

Importance of Clear and Objective Analysis

Engineering work in patent disputes should be:

  • Based on observable data and accepted principles
  • Transparent in its assumptions and methods
  • Independent of advocacy goals
  • Clearly communicated for non-technical audiences

Clarity and objectivity are critical to the usefulness of technical opinions in legal settings.

When Engineering Input May Be Helpful

Engineering analysis may be valuable when:

  • A case involves complex mechanical, electrical, or software systems
  • Technical differences between designs are disputed
  • A product's operation is not easily understood from documentation alone
  • Independent evaluation is needed to support litigation strategy
  • Expert testimony is required

Early technical evaluation can help identify key issues and guide case development.

Missouri Context

In Missouri, Professional Engineers are licensed to provide engineering services within their area of competence. When engaged in patent-related matters, engineers typically work alongside legal counsel and may be asked to provide opinions consistent with their training and experience.

Closing

Patent infringement cases often depend on detailed technical analysis. Professional Engineers can help clarify how systems function, how designs compare, and how technical elements relate to patent claims. This information can assist attorneys and courts in evaluating complex engineering issues.
