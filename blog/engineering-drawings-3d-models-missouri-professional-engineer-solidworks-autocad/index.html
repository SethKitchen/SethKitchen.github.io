Engineering Drawings and 3D Models in Missouri: How a Professional Engineer Can Help Using Tools Like SolidWorks and AutoCAD

April 21, 2026

Engineering projects often begin—and are ultimately executed—based on drawings and models. Whether for permitting, fabrication, construction, or analysis, accurate documentation is essential. In Missouri, Professional Engineers (PEs) may assist not only with technical evaluation but also with preparing or reviewing engineering drawings and 3D models using industry tools such as SolidWorks and AutoCAD.

This article provides general information about how engineering professionals support projects through digital modeling and documentation.

Why Engineering Drawings and Models Matter

Drawings and models communicate how a system is designed, built, and installed. They are used by:

  • Contractors and fabricators
  • Building departments and plan reviewers
  • Inspectors
  • Owners and project stakeholders

Clear documentation helps ensure that work is performed as intended and aligns with project requirements.

2D Drawings vs. 3D Models

2D Drawings (AutoCAD and Similar Tools)

2D drawings typically include:

  • Plans, elevations, and sections
  • Dimensions and tolerances
  • Notes and specifications
  • Details for construction or fabrication

These are often required for permitting and construction.

3D Models (SolidWorks and Similar Tools)

3D models provide a more detailed representation of a system, including:

  • Geometry and spatial relationships
  • Assembly of multiple components
  • Interference or clash detection
  • Visualization of how parts fit together

3D models are commonly used for design development, fabrication, and coordination.

How Professional Engineers Use These Tools

A Professional Engineer may use or review CAD tools to:

  • Develop concept and detailed designs
  • Verify that components fit and function together
  • Evaluate loads, clearances, or constraints
  • Prepare documentation for permitting
  • Communicate design intent to contractors and fabricators

The software is a tool—the engineering judgment behind it is what ensures accuracy and reliability.

Common Applications in Missouri

Engineering drawings and models are used across many types of projects, including:

  • Structural components and connections
  • Mechanical systems and equipment layouts
  • Electrical panel layouts and system diagrams
  • Agricultural equipment and infrastructure
  • Custom fabrication and product design
  • Retrofit or repair details

Both residential and commercial projects may require some level of documentation.

When a Professional Engineer May Be Needed

A PE may be involved when:

  • Drawings are required for permit approval
  • Structural or load-bearing elements are included
  • Custom or non-standard designs are proposed
  • Technical calculations must support the design
  • A sealed set of drawings is required by a jurisdiction
  • The work will be relied upon by contractors or inspectors

In these cases, engineering oversight helps ensure that drawings reflect sound technical principles.

Review vs. Creation of Drawings

Professional Engineers may either:

  • Create drawings and models directly, or
  • Review and approve drawings prepared by others

When reviewing work, the engineer evaluates whether the drawings are consistent with engineering principles and project requirements.

Coordination with Designers and Fabricators

Engineering drawings are often part of a collaborative process involving:

  • Architects and designers
  • Drafters or CAD technicians
  • Contractors and fabricators

Clear coordination helps reduce conflicts, avoid rework, and improve project efficiency.

Benefits of Engineering Involvement

Working with a Professional Engineer can help:

  • Improve accuracy of drawings and models
  • Identify potential issues before construction or fabrication
  • Support permit approval with proper documentation
  • Ensure compliance with applicable codes and standards
  • Provide clarity for installation and inspection

Limitations and Scope

The level of detail and responsibility associated with drawings depends on the scope of engagement. Not all drawings require engineering involvement, and not all CAD work constitutes engineering.

When engineering services are provided, scope, assumptions, and limitations should be clearly defined.

Closing

Engineering drawings and 3D models are essential tools for communicating design intent and supporting construction and fabrication. In Missouri, Professional Engineers may play a role in creating or reviewing these documents when technical accuracy, safety, and compliance are important.
